HOW THE CALL SHOULD GO:

Get to the right person first. If a gatekeeper answers, sound senior and specific: you're trying to reach whoever handles IT or network infrastructure. Don't over-explain.

Once you have the right person, ask for 60 seconds and mean it. Keep the opening tight. If you have a hook from the PRE-CALL INTEL, use it here — something brief and genuine that shows you know who they are.

Your pitch is two sentences max. Lead with what you see happening in their area — firewalls hitting end-of-support, compliance pressure, cyber insurance requirements getting tighter. Fortinet consolidates that. Samson knows the municipal side. That's it. Then stop talking.

Ask one open question and let them go. "How are you handling network security right now — dedicated vendor, or pieced together?" Then listen. Their answer tells you everything. Mirror their language back to them.

If they're engaged: stay on. Ask about their environment, when they last replaced gear, how big their IT team is, whether they're dealing with any compliance requirements. Keep it conversational — you're curious, not interrogating.

If they're short: get to the close fast. Don't over-pitch. Respect their time.

Close for a 15-minute call with Samson, not a demo. "What I'd love to do is connect you with Samson — he covers your area, deals with municipalities specifically. Would a quick 15-minute call this week or next be worth it?" If they want info first, get their email and have Samson follow up directly.

Voicemail: under 20 seconds. Your name, Fortinet, Samson's name, his callback 602-295-0104, one hook — "calling about network security and some end-of-support gear we're seeing in the area." Confident. Done.

"Send info" → "I could, but a 15-minute call with Samson is more useful — he can answer specific questions about your setup. What day works?"

GROUND RULES:
No product names in the opening — lead with pain, not features.
Calls under 3 minutes unless they're clearly engaged.
